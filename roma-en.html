Rome

The Eternal City, where history, art, and life blend beautifully across centuries.

About Rome

Rome, the capital of Italy, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It stands as a living museum — every street, piazza, and ruin tells a story that shaped Western civilization. With a perfect blend of ancient heritage and modern vibrancy, Rome offers a truly timeless experience.

Brief History

Founded in 753 BC, Rome became the heart of the powerful Roman Empire. It was the birthplace of great leaders, philosophers, and architects whose legacy continues to influence the world today. After centuries of change, Rome grew into a global center for religion, culture, and art.

Main Attractions

  • Colosseum – the grand amphitheater that symbolizes ancient Rome’s glory.
  • Roman Forum – ruins of temples and government buildings that once formed the city’s heart.
  • Vatican City – home to St. Peter’s Basilica and the Sistine Chapel with Michelangelo’s frescoes.
  • Trevi Fountain – toss a coin and make a wish to return to Rome!
  • Pantheon – an architectural marvel with a perfect dome, standing for nearly 2000 years.

Culture & Lifestyle

Romans live passionately — food, family, and friendship are at the heart of their culture. The city thrives on its café life, open-air dining, and evening strolls through cobblestone streets. Life in Rome moves at its own rhythm — slow yet full of energy.

Local Cuisine

Italian cuisine in Rome is comforting and rich in flavor. Simplicity is key — fresh ingredients, local olive oil, and handmade pasta.

  • Carbonara – pasta with egg, cheese, and crispy pancetta.
  • Cacio e Pepe – simple yet flavorful pasta with cheese and pepper.
  • Gelato – creamy Italian ice cream with dozens of flavors.
  • Espresso – the Roman way to end every meal.
